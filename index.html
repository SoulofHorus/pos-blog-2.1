Step One: Install MAMP or WAMP.

This seems trivial, but to use phpmyAdmin, you need either MAMP or WAMP. To install MAMP or WAMP, click on those links. These will install and while you wait, move on to the next step.

Note: You can use MAMP on a Windows OS, as I have done.

Step Two: Start up your selected application

Now that you've installed your selected platform. You can launch it. You need to make sure that the ports are set to 80 for your Apache Server and 3306 for your mySQL server. Do not fret if your ports differ from mine, just use whatever your machine says would work.

When lauched it will show 'localhost' as your domain. This means it is only on your machine. Check your Documents folder under users, most likely, or your applications if you are on Mac. The folder you are looking for is htdocs.

Note: You can make multiple folders in your htdocs, just make sure to put in your searchbar 'localhost/[insert-folder-name]' to change where you can view it. Any other files will have to be viewed separately.
